🎮 LifePath Uni – Scenario-Based Decisions
📚 SCENARIO 1: LIBRARY (Study / Discipline / Pressure)
Event 1: “The Silent Grind”

You’ve been studying in the library for 3 hours. Your brain is starting to fog, and your phone keeps lighting up with messages from friends going out tonight.

Choice A: Keep studying

➡️ +Grades, -Energy, -Social
Outcome:
You push through the exhaustion and finish your notes. It’s not fun, but you feel slightly more prepared. Your phone goes silent.

Choice B: Leave and meet friends

➡️ +Social, -Grades, +Energy
Outcome:
You close your laptop and head out. The relief is instant. You’ll deal with the consequences later… hopefully.

Event 2: “The Group Project”

Your group isn’t doing their part. Deadline is tomorrow.

Choice A: Do everything yourself

➡️ +Grades, -Energy, -Health
Outcome:
You stay up all night fixing everything. The project is solid—but you feel completely drained.

Choice B: Confront the group

➡️ +Social (or -Social if things go wrong), +Energy
Outcome:
You send a message calling them out. It’s awkward, but now the responsibility is shared… kind of.

🎉 SCENARIO 2: PARTY (Social / Risk / Impulses)
Event 3: “Basement Party”

You’re at a packed party. Music is loud, drinks are everywhere. You have class tomorrow morning.

Choice A: Stay longer

➡️ +Social, -Energy, -Health
Outcome:
You lose track of time. It’s 4AM before you realize it. Tomorrow is going to hurt.

Choice B: Leave early

➡️ +Energy, -Social
Outcome:
You say your goodbyes and head home. You feel a bit lame… but also responsible.

Event 4: “Peer Pressure”

Someone hands you another drink. You already had a few.

Choice A: Take it

➡️ +Social, -Health
Outcome:
Everyone cheers. You feel included—but slightly out of control.

Choice B: Refuse

➡️ +Health, -Social
Outcome:
You decline. Some people tease you, but deep down you know it was the right call.

🏫 SCENARIO 3: CLASSROOM (Academics / Responsibility)
Event 5: “The Morning Lecture”

Your alarm rings. You slept late.

Choice A: Wake up and go

➡️ +Grades, -Energy
Outcome:
You drag yourself out of bed. You’re tired, but you made it.

Choice B: Skip class

➡️ +Energy, -Grades
Outcome:
You fall back asleep. It feels amazing… until you remember what you missed.

Event 6: “Pop Quiz”

You didn’t study for today.

Choice A: Try your best

➡️ +Grades (small), -Energy
Outcome:
You struggle through it. Not great—but not a total disaster.

Choice B: Cheat

➡️ +Grades (short-term), Risk
Outcome:
You sneak answers. It works… for now. But there’s always a chance of getting caught.

🛏️ SCENARIO 4: DORM / ROOM (Personal Life / Habits)
Event 7: “Late Night Scroll”

You’re in bed, but you’re scrolling endlessly.

Choice A: Keep scrolling

➡️ +Social (minor), -Energy
Outcome:
One more video turns into an hour. You regret it instantly.

Choice B: Sleep

➡️ +Energy, +Health
Outcome:
You put your phone down. Tomorrow-you will thank you.

Event 8: “Messy Room”

Your room is a disaster. Clothes everywhere.

Choice A: Clean up

➡️ +Health, -Energy
Outcome:
It takes time, but your space feels better—and so do you.

Choice B: Ignore it

➡️ +Energy, -Health
Outcome:
You step over the mess. It’s fine… for now.

☕ SCENARIO 5: CAFÉ / DAILY LIFE (Money / Routine)
Event 9: “Coffee Habit”

You pass by your favorite café.

Choice A: Buy coffee

➡️ +Energy, -Money
Outcome:
It tastes amazing. Worth it… right?

Choice B: Skip it

➡️ +Money, -Energy
Outcome:
You walk past. Responsible… but painful.

Event 10: “Part-Time Job Offer”

You’re offered extra hours at work.

Choice A: Accept

➡️ +Money, -Energy, -Grades
Outcome:
More money, less time. You feel the pressure building.

Choice B: Decline

➡️ +Energy, +Grades
Outcome:
You focus on uni. Your wallet isn’t happy, though.

🔥 CHAIN EVENTS (IMPORTANT FOR DEPTH)
Burnout Chain (if Energy < 20)
Event: “Crash”

You physically can’t get out of bed.

Force yourself up → small recovery, big health loss
Stay in bed → recover energy, lose grades
Broke Chain (if Money ≤ 0)
Event: “Card Declined”

Your payment fails.

Ask parents → +Money, -Social
Take loan → +Money, -Health (stress)
